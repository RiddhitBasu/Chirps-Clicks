Tigers of Tadoba

Prelude

This all started very suddenly, on the day before my final exam of the term, when I got the news that my dad and me were going to Tadoba for a couple of nights. Tadoba is a jungle in Maharashtra and one that I have been to before. Not that I remember it as I was too small. I did however remember the tigresses that we had seen (We saw two females, one on the road and one hiding out of sight. Their names were Lara and Maya. In Tadoba there are many core and buffer zones like most jungles. The core zones, which were the zones at the very heart of the jungle, were only open for 8 months from October 1st to July 30th. In this time the small cubs of the tigers are born. So, we had to go to the buffer zone, which is where human settlements (villages) meet the jungle. The buffer is not quite the deep wild jungle but, sightings aren’t very rare. My father has been to Tadoba one time more than I have, besides the many other jungles that he has been to. This blog is about my experience and sightings in Tadoba. So, read on!

Friday, 19th September, 2025

The day started out as a normal school day, as it was the very final exam and a Friday, because of that all my friends and classmates were looking forward to the weekend after the exam. The exam passed by very very sluggishly. After the exam, since it was a half-day, I boarded the bus. The journey wasn’t particularly fun, despite the high spirits after the exams had gotten over, for I was itching to get home. I had done all the tiresome and frankly boring packing work the day before. Today was going to be very hectic, I would get home after coming from school in the annoying traffic and then take a bath, eat lunch at my best friend’s house as I wouldn’t be there for his birthday, and set off to the airport. After an hour and a half ride in the car, we had reached the airport well before time. We had reached so early that the flight before ours in the same gate had only just started boarding. So we sat at the gate. Our flight was to Nagpur, Maharastra also known as the city of oranges as it was famous for them. From there we would take a cab to Tadoba which was about three and a half hours from there. We weren’t going there alone, two of my parents' friends with whom they go to most jungles were coming along too. We waited for them to get through security while we sat at the gate. Then they arrived at the right time, for the boarding started just a few minutes later. It turned out that we were sitting right in the row behind them so going in and coming out wasn’t a hassle. After the two hour journey, we finally reached the city of oranges. From there we got in our cab and strapped on for a long journey. The journey to Tadoba at night was enchanting. The place we were staying in was away from the city and all its pollution of all kinds. As a result I was able to see many stars and the constellations that they formed. The road was pretty good, but it was a bit bumpy here and there. Soon we reached a forest gate into the outskirts of the jungle. From there it took about half an hour to get to our hotel. We reached the hotel and checked in. The hotel was empty because the season had not begun yet, when the tiger cubs would be born, therefore there weren’t many people. Right after we checked in, we decided to eat our dinner and sleep. After all, it had been an exhausting day. We also had to sleep early so we could wake up early for the safari.

Saturday, 20 September, 2025

Part 1

Nothing beats the feeling of waking up excited and curious to see what we could find. It was about 5:00 A.M. and the sun hadn’t come out yet so, it was pitch dark. By the time we got to Dewada gate in the Moharli buffer zone, which is about 5 minutes away from our hotel, the sun had begun to rise. We got into a Gypsy, after a quick ID check. Since it had rained continuously the two weeks before we had arrived, the roads were very slushy. My dad and I were sitting at the very back of the car. We and our parent’s friends would be switching between safaris. We were doing three safaris, two today and one the next morning. Now back to the safari. We had one guide and one driver, like in most jungles. The guide stood up and looked around. The driver was busy maneuvering around the difficult terrain. After a bit of driving, we stopped at a lake in a secluded corner. It was misty around the lake and the sky was a rose gold colour. I took a picture of a tree with peacocks in this backdrop.

Over there, we found pug marks (they are essentially the footprints of wild animals formed when their feet are pressed on a surface) of a tiger. Then we left the secluded corner. The whole road was about 70% water and the other 30% rocky and uneven terrain. Yet both the driver and the car made it look like it was easy crossing all of this. While the driver and guide were busy doing these things, we were busy trying to hold on and the real fun hadn’t even begun yet. We drove down a road with dry trees. Dry bamboo plants were dotted all around the buffer zone. We had to constantly duck down and move around to avoid being hit by their branches, since the car was also pretty tall. Then suddenly we saw a glimpse of black in the thickets. We reversed and saw that it was a lone sloth bear. But it was so deep inside the bushes and thickets that I couldn’t get a focused picture. Even the video I took didn’t focus. But this was our first big sighting of the trip, so I took it in good fashion. We ventured around through a slushy road, there was one part where the soil was so red in colour that it looked like chop suey gravy. So my dad and I named it chopsuey road. After a while of venturing, we stopped at another gate, as I found out later, and ate breakfast provided by the hotel. After that we ventured furthermore but found nothing else. The sloth bear was the main sighting of the first safari. Just like that, we had completed our first safari with a sighting (I honestly didn’t expect to see anything big let alone a tiger, so I was pretty happy about seeing the bear). We got back to the gate where our cab was waiting and hopped onto the cab. We reached the hotel in about 5 minutes. When we did, my father and I realised that there was a flutter of activity around the lawn outside our room. When we took a closer look we realised that they were butterflies. There were about three different colours: brown and white, light blue, and orange. I took a few pictures and videos of them in two different cameras. One for the videos and one for the pictures.

We took some rest for a bit and then took a bath. After that, we went to the restaurant in the hotel to eat lunch. After eating, we returned to our room. We took a short nap and, after waking up and charging our camera batteries, we got ready for the next safari.

Part 2

We took the cab back to the Dewada gate and, after another id check, got onto another Gypsy. This time our guide was a woman and our driver was a man. In the morning, we didn’t exactly go through the Dewada gate and instead through an opening on the main road as the main gate’s road was way too slushy. But this time we went through the main gate as the slush had all dried up. In this safari, We wouldn’t be going to the same part of the jungle. First we went into the jungle and into a place where the village and the jungle met. Here, there were many cattle and cattle are easy prey for any tiger, therefore a tiger was spotted a few days ago. Thinking that there might be a possibility of seeing another tiger here, the guide told the driver to go to this place. We went there positioned ourselves and waited for the tiger. There were two jeeps in front of us. One went very deep into the thickets to see if there was any movement. The other jeep was slightly in front of ours. We waited and waited and waited. There was no sign of anything or any movement. The first jeep that had gone in front had come back saying that there was nothing there. After a lot of waiting both the driver and the guide decided to venture into the jungle and if there happened to be a tiger, which was very unlikely, we would come back. We ventured through chopsuey street again, reminding me that we were still in the same part of the jungle. But that was very short lived as we then went to a place that wasn’t even remotely similar. We passed another village a bit further away from the other one. This village would be close to the place that we were going to stop. We drove into a narrow road near the village, following the driver in front of us. While driving through the jungle we heard a twig or branch break in the thicket as though something stepped on it and broke it. We stopped to wait and see if there was any other sign of movement. We could not see anything in that thicket. The jeep in front of us left long time before, thinking that nothing would be there. We also decided to leave that part. Then after journeying bit far from the spot, our guid had a hunch that the jeep behind us was not coming so, she thought that they must have seen something or they would have been right behind us. So we went back, expectations rising and adrenaline rushing. We went back to the place and already there were many more jeeps than before and that was because there was some activity near that region. Then we saw a flash of orange, white, and black on the left side of the road. By now we were sure there was a tiger there, so we stood up on the jeep’s seats, ready for any signs of activity. And the tiger didn’t disappoint at all. We reversed back to give the tiger space to cross the road and to our expectations he did. And what a sight he was!

He was certainly not bothered by our presence. He crossed the road from the left side to the right. Then he stopped and sniffed a tree a mere few feet from our jeep, probably for the scent of the female tiger (who’s name was Kajri) that was also roaming around this part of the reserve. Shambhu, the male tiger, was a majestic sight. He was young and powerfully built. After giving us a show of his grace, he disappeared into the dense clump of bushes. After the sighting, our guide decided to move deeper near the place we found the tiger. Because of the slush it was difficult to drive around without getting stuck in the quicksand-like mud. For this reason a few cars here and there got stuck. But moreover we had no clue where Shambhu had suddenly vanished. We expected him to appear on the opposite side of the place we had first seen him. But he was nowhere to be seen. We waited and waited (again) but, as I learned as an important lesson, patience was a key here. After waiting for a long time, the sun began to go down and the light was dimming, so we decided to turn around and go back to the gate. From there we took our cab back to the hotel. Today was my best friend”s birthday so I got a video call from him when his birthday cake-cutting was going on after we reached the hotel. I wished him and told him that I would have a full conversation with him when I’d get back home. We were going to have dinner early as we had to get early for tomorrow’s safari.

Sunday, 21 September, 2025

We woke up early at 5:00 again for our last safari. This safari would take place in a different gate as the ones the day before. When we got there I realised that the gate was the same one as the one that we stopped for breakfast the day before one that first safari. It led us to pretty much the same place as the evening safari. So naturally we were looking for the same tiger and if we were lucky the female too. We went to the slushy place again and we went to a barricaded part of this road (it was barricaded so the villagers don’t accidentally go into the inner part of the forest, but all the safari cars were allowed to go there) and into a part which didn’t look like the rest of the forest as there were no trees here. Then we spotted him, Shambhu, resting in a water body on that very road.

I took a few pictures of him and then I took a video of him just sitting there and drinking water on that considerably hot day. Then he got up and out of the pond and started walking on the side of the road. After chasing him for a while he disappeared again. We roamed around the pond for a while until we saw a cluster of jeeps all clearly looking at something that was in an indent in the ground. If our luck was good that would be Shambhu again but it turned out to be even better than that. It was actually Kajri, the female, who was clearly disturbed by the jeeps surrounding her. So she went into a thicket. During this time we decided that, while we waited, we would eat some breakfast. After eating, we decided to venture around a bit and asleep in a thicket barely visible was our favourite, Shambhu. Then after that, we saw that the jeeps began to move and disturbed by the jeeps Kajri decided to climb out of the indent and started to walk toward Shambhu, where we were waiting for him to get up. Then Kajri went right beside him and Shambhu, agitated at being woken up from his slumber, chased her away. Kajri retreated from there and began to walk on the road where I finally got a few pictures of Kajri.

After the encounter both of them vanished into the deep thickets of the forest. We waited, and waited just so we could see if they would come out. Even after all that waiting the two did not come out so, we headed back to the gate. From there we took our cab back to the hotel, ate lunch there, checked out and got ready for the journey back. After another three and a half hour drive we reached Nagpur where we stopped for a short snack break and to buy a few presents for my mother and sister back at home. From there it took about 30 minutes to reach the airpport. We went through security, got to the gate and hopped aboard the flight back home.

Conclusion

This trip was both a good one for sightings and one for taking some good pictures. But what I personally enjoyed on this trip was the jungle and its serenity. I also really liked taking some videos of the backdrop with the contrast of the blue sky and the green jungle. In the jungle itself there was a lot of biodiversity, like the lush green forest to the dry patch of land were we found Shambhu sitting in the water. The jungle was a place where human settlements, here the village, met the wild jungle and its serene beauty.
